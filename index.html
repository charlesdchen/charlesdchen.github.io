Hi, I'm Charlie

I'm a PhD candidate in Imaging Science at Washington University in St. Louis. I study imaging techniques like MRI, which are powerful tools for analyzing the human brain. However, they cannot truly resolve microsopic tissue structures of interest. My work compares imaging results with microscopy findings to better understand what MRI and other imaging techniques are measuring in the brain.

Outside of research, I enjoy writing essays, playing music, and answering questions that pop into my head and don't go away, among other things.

Projects

Amyloid-β

Pittsburgh Compound-B (PiB) positron emission tomography (PET) is used for identifying the presence of amyloid-β in the brain. However, PiB PET cannot distinguish diffuse and cored/compact plaques, which are two sources of amyloid-β in Alzheimer disease (AD). Here we compared in vivo PiB PET with follow-up stereology of amyloid-β plaques in individuals with typical AD and those with a rare dominantly inherited form of AD (DIAD). We found that the PiB PET correlates with diffuse plaque burden in DIAD and with both diffuse and cored/compact plaque burdens in typical AD.

Tau

Flortaucipir positron emission tomography (PET) is used for identifying the presence of tau in the brain. However, flortaucipir PET cannot distinguish neurofibrillary tangles, neuritic plaques, and neuropil threads, which are three sources of tau in Alzheimer disease (AD). Here we compared in vivo flortaucipir PET in individuals with typical AD and those with a rare dominantly inherited form of AD (DIAD) with stereology of tau burden in a different cohort. We found that flortaucipir PET reflects total tau burden in both typical AD and DIAD, composed mainly of neurofibrillary tangle and neuropil thread burdens.

  • 2020

Myelin

Diffusion MRI and probablistic tractography are powerful tools for analyzing long-distance connections within the myelinated white matter of the brain. However, there is a tendency for tractography streamlines to terminate preferentially at gyral crowns versus sulcal banks and fundi, resulting in a folding-related bias in apparent connectivity. Here we characterize this gyral bias by comparing diffusion MRI fiber orientations with axonal trajectories estimated from myelin-stained histology sections. We found that in sulcal banks and fundi, fibers crossing the gray/white boundary were less heavily myelinated than at gyral crowns, resulting in less accurate diffusion MRI fiber estimates.

Side projects

What happened on this day in US history?

This is my attempt at filling a calendar with important events from US history. I thought it could make for a fun way to learn a little bit about US history over time. I've tried to get an even sampling of various kinds of history, not just military. Still, this is a work in progress, the choice of events to highlight remains very subjective, and inevitably there will be several important events missing, and several movements or eras that cannot easily be distilled into one watershed moment with a known date and time. The calendar is based on this design by Dudley Storey, and I've added some code to handle the differently-sized photos coming from external sites. Photo credit: Margaret Bourke-White.
